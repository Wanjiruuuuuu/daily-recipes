Gullan Red Rice

Gullah red rice is one of Charleston, South Carolina's best dishes. Being from the sea islands of Charleston, this is a go-to dish passed down!

INGREDIENTS

  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 smoked sausage links, sliced
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce (such as Hunt's®)
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 cups uncooked parboiled (converted) rice
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder

STEPS

  1. Place bacon in a pot and cook over medium-high heat, turning occasionally, until evenly browned (7-10 minutes).
  2. Add bell pepper and onion to the bacon grease and sauté over medium heat until soft (about 5 minutes).
  3. Add sausage and cook, stirring, until browned (8-10 minutes).
  4. Remove the bacon, bell pepper, onion, and sausage mixture from the pan and set aside in a bowl.
  5. Pour tomato sauce, tomato paste, and 1 cup of water into the same pot.
  6. Bring to a boil and add rice, sugar, salt, pepper, onion powder, and garlic powder.
  7. Bring the mixture back to a boil, reduce heat, and let it simmer over medium-low heat for 10 minutes.
  8. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  9. Transfer the rice mixture to a 9x13-inch baking pan and cover it with foil.
  10. Bake in the preheated oven until the liquid has evaporated and the rice is tender (about 25 minutes).
